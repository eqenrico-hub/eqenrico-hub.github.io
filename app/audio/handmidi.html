What It Does

HandMIDI uses your webcam to track hand movements in real time and converts them into MIDI messages. Move your hand up to increase a filter cutoff, spread your fingers to open a reverb, pinch to trigger a note. No hardware needed — just your hands and a camera.

Available on: Gumroad (Ritual Audio Labs)

Problem It Solves

MIDI controllers are expensive and inflexible — each knob does one thing. HandMIDI gives you continuous, expressive control over any parameter using natural hand movements. It's especially useful for live performance, sound design experiments, and accessibility (no physical hardware required).

Key Features

  • Real-time hand tracking — MediaPipe hand landmarks via webcam
  • Multi-gesture mapping — map different gestures (pinch, spread, rotate, height) to different MIDI CCs
  • Works with any DAW — sends standard MIDI via virtual MIDI port
  • Low latency — optimized for live performance
  • License key system — Gumroad integration for distribution

Tech Stack

Python MediaPipe OpenCV python-rtmidi Gumroad API

Why I Built This

I wanted to experiment with gesture-based music control without buying expensive hardware. MediaPipe made hand tracking accessible, and the jump from "hand position" to "MIDI CC value" was surprisingly natural. It started as a weekend hack and became a product on Gumroad through Ritual Audio Labs.
