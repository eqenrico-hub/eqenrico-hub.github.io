What It Does

An interactive dashboard that analyzes Freedom of Information (FOI) request data from the Belgian transparency platform (transparencia.be). It measures how responsive different public administrations are to citizen information requests over a 10-year period (2016-2025).

Problem It Solves

While FOI laws exist to ensure government transparency, there's little systematic analysis of how well administrations actually respond. This dashboard provides empirical evidence of accountability performance, helping researchers and policymakers understand patterns in government responsiveness.

Key Features

  • Response Time Analysis - Track how quickly administrations respond
  • Completion Rates - Measure successful vs. pending/rejected requests
  • Trend Visualization - See changes in responsiveness over time
  • Cross-Administration Comparison - Compare different public bodies
  • Sentiment Analysis - Analyze tone of requests and responses

Why I Built This

This is part of my PhD research at the University of Genoa in collaboration with ULB Brussels. The goal is to operationalize accountability as a measurable organizational practice, moving beyond abstract definitions to concrete, evidence-based analysis.

Academic Context

This research contributes to public sector management literature by proposing FOI responsiveness as a proxy for organizational accountability orientation. The work is being prepared for submission to academic journals including Azienda Pubblica.
