Paper

"Everyone is equal before the law": Assessing gendered information needs and administrative responsiveness

Julia Trautendorfer (JKU Linz), Enrico Quaglia (University of Genoa / ULB Brussels), Giovanni Esposito (ULB Brussels), Maximilien Mabillard (ULB Brussels)

Submitted to ENPA Inaugural Conference 2026 — "Shaping the Future of Public Administration in Europe Together"
Bucharest University of Economic Studies, Romania — 22–25 April 2026

What This Paper Does

This paper investigates whether gender — of both the requester and the responding official — shapes how public administrations handle Freedom of Information (FOI) requests. Using 4,265 real FOI requests submitted via Belgium's Transparencia.be platform (2016–2025), it tests whether the democratic promise of equal treatment holds in practice.

Research Questions

  • Do public administrations respond differently to FOI requests depending on the requester's gender?
  • Does the respondent's gender influence how requests are handled?
  • Do interactions between requester and respondent gender create systematic patterns of bias?

Key Findings

  • 1,215 FOI requests with known requester AND respondent gender analyzed via multinomial logistic regression
  • Three outcome variables: response likelihood, response time, and information completeness
  • Evidence of discretionary patterns in how bureaucrats process requests based on gender signals
  • Contributes to street-level bureaucracy theory (Lipsky, 2010; Chang & Brewer, 2023) and feminist institutionalism (Durose & Lowndes, 2024)

My Contribution

As second author, I was responsible for:

  • Methodology design — built the entire 5-tier gender classification pipeline (see below)
  • Data preparation — scraped, cleaned, and structured 4,265 FOI request folders (10,002 outgoing + 7,088 incoming documents)
  • Quantitative analysis — topic modeling, sentiment analysis, statistical models
  • 3 interactive dashboards — prototypes for citizens, researchers, and public administrations

Gender Classification Pipeline

The core methodological contribution is a 5-tier deterministic + LLM cascade that classifies requester and respondent gender from unstructured FOI text:

  • Tier 0 — Response greeting detection ("Monsieur"/"Madame") → perceived gender (takes precedence)
  • Tier 1 — Larousse dictionary lookup (8,712 French names)
  • Tier 2 — Statbel Belgian registry (8,146 additional names)
  • Tier 3 — Combined Larousse + Statbel with gender agreement
  • Tier 4 — Qwen 2.5 LLM fallback (7.6B, locally deployed via Ollama)

Results: 3,825 requesters classified M/F (89.7%) • 1,293 respondents classified M/F • 1,201 known-sex pairs for regression analysis
